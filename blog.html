Digital transformation

FPT CEO: 'In Digital Transformation, People are Crucial'

     According to Nguyen Van Khoa, when it comes to digital transformation, organizations and businesses face numerous barriers such as corporate culture and transparency. They may also need to dismantle old business models to create new ones. One of the biggest challenges is transforming people and their mindsets...

     Perhaps in the future, 2020 will be remembered for two significant events: Covid-19 and the wave of digital transformation. These two events simultaneously brought about unprecedented drastic and harsh changes in all fields globally.

     Covid-19 led to social distancing, while digital transformation created a new kind of connectivity: connecting minds, data, society, resources, and production materials in a completely different way, which was previously only seen in science fiction movies.

     In an interview with the press before the new year, Nguyen Van Khoa, CEO of FPT Corporation, one of the pioneers in Vietnam’s national digital transformation campaign, believes that digital transformation is not a fantasy about a destination but a mandatory journey if business owners do not want to see their enterprises disappear.

Digital Transformation in 2020:

        • 2020 will go down in history with the Covid-19 crisis and the digital transformation trend. As the CEO of a technology corporation expected to lead Vietnam's digital transformation journey, how do you view the past year: an opportunity or a challenge? What has FPT done to adapt and respond to these impacts?
‍
     Many of FPT's digital transformation solutions and services have been recognized internationally: for instance, akaBot, a business process automation solution, was honored as one of the top 6 RPA solutions globally. Domestically, many leading companies in fisheries, tourism, telecommunications, manufacturing, real estate, etc., have trusted FPT as their comprehensive digital transformation consultant.

Challenges and Strategies:

        • Never before has the natural push towards digital transformation been as strong as it was last year. For businesses to survive and thrive in what you call "wartime," they need coordinated action. What have you and FPT done to support the Vietnamese business community in this harsh battle?
‍
     In "wartime," on the economic front, we achieved a crucial task: forming alliances and collaborating with the business community. I have been directly leading digital transformation projects for key organizations and major corporations in sectors like fisheries, real estate, telecommunications, energy, tourism, and aviation...

FPT’s Initiatives:

     • Vietnam has many businesses that started digital transformation early but were unsuccessful and sought FPT's consultation. Drawing from international experiences and valuable lessons, we have advised them on why their digital transformation failed, highlighting limitations and critical points that businesses dared not address in management, operations, production, and business...

Supporting Digital Transformation:

      • In the realm of digital society and digital government, with deep understanding and over 20 years of experience implementing significant national technology systems, FPT continues to partner with the government, ministries, and localities to offer strategic directions and digital transformation programs in key areas like finance, banking, healthcare, education...

Future Outlook:

       •  FPT has ambitious plans for 2021. In this decade, FPT aims to rank among the Top 50 global digital transformation service companies. Domestically, we will lead the national digital transformation process, providing distinct values to people and businesses. With the growing demand for digital transformation, expected to increase by 18-25% annually, FPT is confident in its leading role in driving national digital transformation.

Conclusion:

     Digital transformation not only offers a new direction for business operations but also holds the potential to revolutionize various sectors, contributing to national development and sustainability.
