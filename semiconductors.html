SEMICONDUCTORS

FPT's Environmental Impact and Sustainability Initiatives

    Sharing at the seminar "Semiconductor Chip Design: New Opportunities - Future," organized by FPT Jetking in collaboration with the National Innovation Center (NIC), Mr. Hoang Nam Tien, Vice Chairman of the FPT University Council (FPT Education), stated that FPT Group (stock code FPT - HoSE exchange) will export 70 million semiconductor chips to Japan and South Korea.

     These chips are specifically for medical devices and electronic applications, expected to be delivered to customers this year and by 2025. Mr. Hoang Nam Tien also mentioned that these chip models are designed by FPT Semiconductor, a division specializing in semiconductors within FPT Group. After design, they are manufactured in South Korea and packaged in Taiwan.

     Currently, FPT Semiconductor and Viettel High Tech are the only Vietnamese enterprises capable of semiconductor chip design. These companies are also moving towards autonomous production and packaging of chips in the future.

     Regarding the prospects of semiconductor industry development, Mr. Hoang Nam Tien believes that within just 5 years, Vietnam will be prominently recognized in the global semiconductor industry. He explained this view by highlighting the Vietnamese people's perseverance and patience in work and learning. Vietnamese youth are adept at self-learning new knowledge and learn quickly.
Integrating STEM education (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) into school curricula from grades 1 to 12 will be the foundation for future chip designers.

     Regarding FPT Jetking's semiconductor design training program, the Vice Chairman of FPT University Council believes that students can start working in semiconductor facilities after just 4 semesters (14-16 months).
Speaking at the seminar, Mr. Harsh Bharwani, CEO of Jetking Group (with over 75 years in education and technology training), stated that the global chip market is currently valued at $500 billion USD and is expected to nearly quadruple to $1.8 trillion USD in the coming years, driving substantial demand for talent.

     Mr. Harsh Bharwani expects that training students in Vietnam to serve this billion-dollar industry will have great potential in the next 10 years.
"Vietnam has many strengths in chip design with its young population and strong development. The youth here have knowledge and rapid technology adoption. To meet the demands of large manufacturers, they will need to improve in areas like English proficiency, global economic outlook, new chip design technologies, and artificial intelligence. Additionally, mastering both theory and practical skills is crucial in learning any technology," Mr. Harsh Bharwani added.

     Experts attending the seminar agree that FPT Group's large-scale export of semiconductor chips to South Korea and Japan will create significant opportunities for Vietnam to gradually become a crucial link in the global semiconductor supply chain.
